Introduction to Soccer

Soccer, also known as football or association football, is a sport played between two teams of eleven players for each team. Soccer is also known as the King of Sports in many countries. Gradually speaking, soccer has been growing since the last 50 years, where people all over the world is starting to see soccer from another perspective. Although it is not very famous in some countries such as Canada or United States, where the sport just started to take over in the recent years due to the fact that famous and good soccer players are coming and playing in the Mayor League Soccer(MLS). Other countries such as Spain which has "La Liga BBVA"(Spain's soccer league) where most of the best players in the world plays, it is also the most popular and important league out of all five big European Soccer Nations (England, Italy, Germany, France, Spain).

Main Rules

Like on any sports, rules are very important and they many of them. In order to play the sport, people should know the following:

  1. There can only be eleven player per teams, only two teams play against each other at the same time.
  2. A maximun of three substitutions are allowed on Official game matches such as League games and Cup matches, only on International friendly matches and on pre-season matches are allowed to have from five to seven substitutions.
  3. The match total duration is ninety (90) minutes, which are played in two halfs of forty five(45) minutes with fifteen (15) minutes of half break.
  4. There should be no aggressions to other player. If a player commits a foul, depending on the degree of aggression or how the fault was done, the referee can gives the player either a warning, a "Yellow Card" or a "Red Card". Yellow cards are not as important as red cards, the difference between them are:
    1. A yellow card gives a player a warning that if another foul of the same degree is committed than he'll be booked with a second yellow card which will be equal to having a red card.
    2. On international Friendly matches, yellow card are 'NOT' accumulated. But, on leagues and cup matches, yellow card are accumulated. If a player accumulates four yellow cards gets a one match suspension.
    3. Red card will send the player automatically off the field leaving the team with ten player, no substitution can replace the player dismissed, and also he will miss the next match. A team with more than five red cards in one match, will be declare as a automatic loose to that team.
